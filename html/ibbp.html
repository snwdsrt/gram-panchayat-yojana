Karnataka Trade License

The Shop and Establishment Act lays down various stipulations, including working hours, employment norms, paid leave regulations, and responsibilities between employers and employees.

Features of Trade License

Trade licenses ensure that businesses comply with safety regulations, preventing potential harm to the public.

  • Why Trade License?: To safeguard society from nuisance and health hazards posed by improper business practices.
  • Who Needs a Trade License?: Every trader involved in general trading within municipal corporation boundaries needs a license.
  • Licensing Authority: Issued by the municipal corporation in consultation with other departments such as health, fire brigade, and engineering.
  • Commercial Premises: A trade license can only be obtained for businesses conducted in approved areas (commercial, mixed land use, etc.).

Businesses Requiring a Trade License

As per the Karnataka Municipal Corporation Act, 1976, a trade license is mandatory for specific business activities. Businesses must apply for a license at least 30 days before initiating trade.

Examples of Businesses Requiring a Trade License:

  • Eating establishments like hotels, restaurants, and bakeries.
  • Manufacturing industries, power looms, and factories.
  • Offensive and dangerous trades like a dhobi shop, barbershop, and fireworks manufacturers.

Documents Required

To obtain a Karnataka trade license, the following documents need to be submitted along with the application:

  • Identity and address proof of the business owner.
  • Passport size photograph of the applicant.
  • Approval documents for the building and plant permit.
  • Property documents such as property tax receipts or rental agreements.
  • NOC from the premises owner and Pollution Control Board.
  • Details of machinery or power used in the premises.
  • Katha Extract, a property tax assessment document.
