Mahila Kayakotsava Scheme

Mahila Kayakotsava, also known as the Women's Empowerment Campaign or Mahila Udyoga Sabaleekarana Abhiyana, is an initiative under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) aimed at enhancing the participation of women and gender minorities in rural employment.

Objectives of the Campaign

  • Increase women's participation rate to at least 60% under MGNREGS.
  • Provide Job Cards and unskilled work to gender minorities under the scheme.
  • Offer economic empowerment to women and gender minorities by providing individual facilities under MGNREGS.
  • Create women and child-friendly workplaces.
  • Encourage participation of Self-Help Groups (SHGs) under MGNREGS and train Women Kayaka Bandhus to motivate increased participation.
  • Promote SHG involvement in village markets and organize sales fairs for daily-use items on non-market days.

Preparation and Implementation

The campaign involves several preparatory activities:

  • Conduct meetings with departments associated with women's welfare, SHG federations at the Gram Panchayat level, and various stakeholders.
  • Create awareness about the campaign through pamphlets, local media, and announcements about conducting Mahila Gram Sabhas (women's village assemblies).
  • Inform women and gender minorities in SHGs about the campaign and ensure active participation in Mahila Gram Sabhas.
  • Have government departments prepare details about various schemes available for women's empowerment to be shared during the Gram Sabha.

Organizing Mahila Gram Sabhas

Key steps include:

  • Conduct Gram Panchayat level Mahila Gram Sabhas on dates fixed by Taluk Panchayat Executive Officers at designated locations.
  • Ensure attendance of elected Gram Panchayat members and local departmental officers.
  • Ensure participation of all gender minorities within Gram Panchayat limits.
  • Provide information about women's rights, the purpose of MGNREGA, workplace safety, childcare facilities, wages, and other amenities.
  • Share details about government schemes and facilities available for women's empowerment.
  • Organize general health check-up camps, especially anemia check-ups, by Primary Health Center medical officers during the Gram Sabha.
  • Offer opportunities for weaker sections, women, specially-abled individuals, and gender minorities to express their demands in the Gram Sabha.
  • Set up a "Gram Panchayat Desk" at the Gram Sabha venue with brochures containing information on facilities available to women and gender minorities from various government departments.
  • Identify unregistered women and gender minorities under the MGNREGS, register them on the spot, and issue Job Cards.
  • Accept job demand forms (Form-6) from registered women and gender minorities and provide work within 15 days of receiving the demand.
  • Accept demands for individual works from registered women and gender minorities, approve them in the Gram Sabha, and include them in the action plan.
  • Provide space in the Gram Sabha for display and sale of locally made products by SHGs, women's empowerment societies, and local women.

Implementation and Monitoring

Gram Panchayat

  • Presidents and Panchayat Development Officers should take steps to conduct Mahila Gram Sabhas.
  • Inform local officials, SHGs, Sanjeevini program staff, and local women's and youth associations about the date, time, and venue of the Mahila Gram Sabha.
  • Document photos, videos, and proceedings of the Mahila Gram Sabha and submit information to superiors within the stipulated time, recording it in the Panchatantra software.

Taluk Panchayat

  • Taluk Panchayat Executive Officers act as Nodal Officers for their Taluk's campaign.
  • Organize workshops on training registration, duration, location, and benefits in coordination with Project Managers of NRLM through Project Implementing Agencies related to skill training (DDUGKY) before organizing Mahila Gram Sabhas.
  • Fix dates for Mahila Gram Sabhas, publish the schedule, and communicate them to the concerned Gram Panchayats.
  • Ensure Mahila Udyoga Sabaleekarana Abhiyana and Mahila Gram Sabhas take place in all Gram Panchayats of their taluk, assigning a Nodal Officer to each Gram Panchayat for effective implementation.
  • Direct local departmental officials to be present on the day of the Mahila Gram Sabha.
  • Organize exhibitions and sales fairs of products made by SHGs through the Taluk Panchayat.

Zilla Panchayat

  • Chief Executive Officers at the district level serve as Nodal Officers for the campaign.
  • Identify voluntary organizations in the field of Women & Child Development in the district and involve them in implementing government schemes in Gram Panchayats.
  • Motivate activities and programs during the campaign to make Gram Panchayats more women-employment friendly.
  • Organize exhibitions and sales fairs to promote products produced by SHGs.
  • Coordinate with NRLM for training programs, organizing workshops on training registration, duration, location, and benefits through YouTube live sessions to ensure women's participation.
