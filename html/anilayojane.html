Anila Yojane

Anila Yojane, also known as the National Biogas and Organic Manure Programme (NNBOMP), is a centrally sponsored scheme implemented by the Government of India since 1982-83. The program aims to promote the use of biogas as a clean, non-polluting, smoke and soot-free fuel produced from cattle dung, human waste, and organic matter through anaerobic digestion. The resultant slurry from the biogas plant serves as high-quality manure for agricultural fields. This initiative is primarily targeted towards women, aiming to provide them with sustainable energy solutions and improve their quality of life.

Objectives

  • Provide clean cooking fuel for households, reducing dependence on traditional biomass fuels.
  • Produce enriched organic manure to enhance soil fertility and agricultural productivity.
  • Mitigate drudgery of women and save time for other livelihood activities.
  • Improve sanitation in rural and semi-urban areas by linking sanitary toilets with biogas plants.
  • Contribute to climate change mitigation by reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Beneficiaries

The program is primarily targeted towards women, aiming to empower them by providing access to clean energy and improving their socio-economic status.

Benefits

  • Installation of biogas plants ranging from 1 to 25 cubic meters per day capacity.
  • Access to clean cooking fuel, reducing indoor air pollution and health hazards.
  • Production of high-quality organic manure, reducing dependence on chemical fertilizers.
  • Reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to climate change mitigation.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Beneficiary should have their own land/space of about 50-60 square meters for installing a small biogas plant.
  • Availability of cow dung/feedstock and regular water supply.
  • Financial capacity to invest their own share of money for the biogas plant.

Central Financial Assistance (CFA)

The program provides financial assistance based on the capacity of the biogas plant and the beneficiary's category. For instance, general category beneficiaries in all other states receive Rs. 7,500 for a 1 cubic meter plant and Rs. 12,000 for a 2-6 cubic meter plant. Additional subsidies are available for cattle dung-based biogas plants linked with sanitary toilets for individual households.

How to Apply

Interested individuals can apply for the Anila Yojane through the official website of the Rural Development and Panchayat Raj Department of Karnataka: rdpr.kar.nic.in/English/AnilaYojane.asp.
