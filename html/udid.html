Unique Disability Identity (UDID) Scheme

The Unique Disability Identity (UDID) Scheme is an initiative by the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD) under the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Government of India. Launched with the aim of creating a national database for Persons with Disabilities (PwDs), the scheme seeks to issue a Unique Disability Identity Card to each person with disabilities, promoting transparency, efficiency, and ease of access to government benefits.

Objectives of the UDID Scheme

  • Establish a national database for PwDs to streamline the tracking of physical and financial progress of beneficiaries at all levels—from village to national.
  • Issue a Unique Disability Identity Card to each person with disabilities, ensuring uniformity and ease of delivering government benefits.
  • Encourage transparency and efficiency in the implementation of schemes and benefits meant for PwDs.

Key Features of the UDID Project

  • Online availability of data of Persons with Disabilities across the country through a centralized web application.
  • Online filing and submission of registration application forms for disability certificates/Universal ID cards; offline applications may also be accepted and subsequently digitized by agencies.
  • Quick assessment process for calculating the percentage of disability by hospitals/Medical Boards.
  • Prevention of duplication of PwD data.
  • Online renewal and update of information by Persons with Disabilities or on their behalf.
  • Management Information System (MIS) reporting framework.
  • Effective management, including interoperability of the benefits/schemes launched by the Government for PwDs.
  • Provision to accommodate additional disabilities in the future, as per new acts or notifications.

Benefits of the UDID Card

  • Eliminates the need for multiple documents; the card captures all necessary details, which can be decoded with the help of a reader.
  • Serves as a single document for identification and verification, simplifying the process of availing various benefits in the future.
  • Assists in streamlining the tracking of physical and financial progress of beneficiaries at all levels of implementation—from village to national.

Types of Disabilities Covered

As per the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the UDID scheme covers the following types of disabilities:

  • Blindness
  • Low vision
  • Leprosy cured persons
  • Hearing impairment (deaf and hard of hearing)
  • Locomotor disability
  • Dwarfism
  • Intellectual disability
  • Mental illness
  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Cerebral palsy
  • Muscular dystrophy
  • Chronic neurological conditions
  • Specific learning disabilities
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Speech and language disability
  • Thalassemia
  • Hemophilia
  • Sickle cell disease
  • Multiple disabilities including deafblindness
  • Acid attack victims
  • Parkinson's disease

Application Process for UDID Card

  1. Persons with Disabilities (PwDs) need to register on the UDID portal: https://www.swavlambancard.gov.in/.
  2. After registration, log in to the system and click on "Apply online for Disability Certificate." Read the instructions and fill up the online application form.
  3. Upload a color passport photo and other requisite documents like Income Proof, Identity Proof, and SC/ST/OBC proof as required.
  4. Submit the data to the Chief Medical Officer (CMO) Office/Medical Authority.
  5. The CMO Office/Medical Authority verifies the data and assigns the concerned specialist(s) for assessment.
  6. The specialist doctor assesses the disability of the PwD and provides an opinion on the disability.
  7. The Medical Board reviews the case and assigns a disability percentage. The CMO Office prepares the Disability Certificate and generates the UDID and Disability Certificate.
  8. The UDID datasheet is processed for UDID Card printing, and the card is dispatched to the PwD.
