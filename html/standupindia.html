Stand Up India Scheme

The Stand Up India Scheme is an initiative by the Government of India to promote entrepreneurship among Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and women entrepreneurs. Launched on April 5, 2016, the scheme aims to facilitate bank loans ranging from ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore for setting up greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors. Each bank branch is expected to support at least one SC/ST borrower and one woman borrower.

Objectives

  • Promote entrepreneurship among SC/ST and women entrepreneurs.
  • Facilitate bank loans for greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors.
  • Provide handholding support to borrowers at both pre-loan and operational stages.

Key Features

  • Loans between ₹10 lakh and ₹1 crore for greenfield projects.
  • Repayment period of up to 7 years with a maximum moratorium period of 18 months.
  • Composite loan covering 75% of the project cost, including term loan and working capital.
  • Collateral-free loans backed by Credit Guarantee Fund for Stand Up India (CGFSI).
  • Margin money requirement of up to 25%, with possible convergence with state schemes to reduce actual margin money needed.

Eligibility Criteria

  • SC/ST and/or women entrepreneurs above 18 years of age.
  • Loans available only for greenfield projects, indicating the first-time venture of the beneficiary in the manufacturing, services, or trading sector.
  • In case of non-individual enterprises, at least 51% of the shareholding and controlling stakes should be held by either SC/ST and/or women entrepreneurs.
  • Borrowers should not be in default to any bank or financial institution.

Implementation Mechanism

  • Refinance window through Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) with an initial amount of ₹10,000 crore.
  • Creation of a credit guarantee mechanism through the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC).
  • Handholding support for borrowers, including increasing familiarity with factoring services, registration with online platforms, e-marketplaces, and sessions on best practices and problem-solving.

Application Process

The scheme can be accessed in three ways:

  1. Directly at a bank branch.
  2. Through the SIDBI Stand-Up India Portal: www.standupmitra.in
  3. Through the Lead District Manager.

Benefits

The Stand Up India Scheme caters to promoting entrepreneurship among women, SC, and ST categories—sections of the population facing significant hurdles due to lack of advice/mentorship and inadequate and delayed credit. The scheme intends to leverage the institutional credit structure to reach out to these underserved sectors of the population in starting greenfield enterprises. It caters to both ready and trainee borrowers. To extend collateral-free coverage, the Government of India has set up the Credit Guarantee Fund for Stand Up India (CGFSI). Apart from providing credit facilities, the scheme also envisages extending handholding support to potential borrowers.
